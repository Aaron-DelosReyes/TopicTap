Overview of Communication Models

Communication is a complex process.

A model is used to depict the complex nature of communication.

Models are used to depict ideas and thoughts.

Digital Grants Traffic Model

The concept of a digital grants traffic model of communication is simplified into a process of provisioning a visual representation in the different aspects of communication.

Counters describe the process of communication visually, showing the relationship among the elements and helping find and correct communication problems.

Understanding Communication Components

Understanding the important components of communication is important.

Linear Model of Communication

  • The linear model is a one-way process.
  • It presents simple communication.
  • The transmission of the message shows only the beginning, and there is no exchange of roles between the sender and receiver.

Interactive Model of Communication

This model deals with exchange.

This model incorporates speed and feedback, which makes communication a more interactive two-way process.

Internet Communication Model

This is a basic illustration of the internet, focusing on the interaction and less on the message.

Assets concern a communication process as a whole, items to form bonds and relationships among people.

The commonality of experience between the sender and the receiver makes the communication process more engaging.

Transactional Model of Communication

The transactional model illustrates that both the sender and receiver of a message must be actively engaged at the same time to establish communication.

Transactional communication can be ineffective if the person receiving the message is unable to listen to what the sender is saying.

Hills Helix Model

In 1967, inspired by a helix, a three-dimensional spiral-like shape of a cylinder, the Hills helix was created.
