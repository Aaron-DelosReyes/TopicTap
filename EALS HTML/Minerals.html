Understanding Minerals

Minerals are the building blocks of rocks and come in a wide variety of colors, shapes, sizes, textures, and physical characteristics. There are over 2,000 varieties of minerals on Earth. Diamonds, fluorite, talc, and selenite gypsum are some examples of minerals used in various applications.

Criteria for Minerals

For a substance to be considered a mineral, it must meet specific criteria:

  1. Must exist as a solid under normal conditions on Earth.
  2. Must be naturally occurring on Earth.
  3. Must be inorganic (not coming from living or made of living things).
  4. Must have a fixed chemical formula (made up of a specific combination of elements).
  5. Atoms must be arranged in an orderly crystal structure.

Substances like mercury and coal do not qualify as minerals, while ice is debated as a mineral due to its varying states. However, sulfur meets all the criteria and is considered a mineral.

Physical Properties

All physical properties of a mineral result from the internal arrangement of atoms.

For example, sulfur appears yellow, quartz can be clear, and halite tastes salty due to their atomic arrangement.

Diamond is the hardest mineral, while graphite, used in pencils, is much softer due to its atomic structure.
