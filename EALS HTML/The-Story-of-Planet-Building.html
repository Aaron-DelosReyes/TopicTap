The Story of Earth's Formation and the Journey of Asteroid Bennu

This is the story of our Earth’s formation four-and-a-half billion years ago, and of a little asteroid called Bennu, who’s also survived until now. We have the team at NASA to thank for bringing us this video.

Origin of the Solar System

The Milky Way, home to billions of stars, rising and setting over billions of worlds, including our own. In this vast expanse, how did our Sun, the Earth, and the planets come to be? In recent decades, our understanding of the solar system’s evolution has greatly improved, but deep questions remain. To answer those questions, astronomers are preparing to visit someplace very small: asteroid Bennu.

Bennu: A Time Capsule

Bennu is a lump of rock and organic material, the early building blocks of the solar system—of Earth, of us. Bennu is a time capsule, and its journey takes us way, way back, four-and-a-half billion years.

Formation of the Solar System

  • The raw ingredients of Bennu and our solar system originated in a stellar nursery—a vast cloud of hydrogen, helium, and dust.
  • Nearby hot stars quickly burn up their fuel and destroy themselves in colossal explosions called supernovae, destabilizing our cloud and causing it to collapse.
  • In a geologic blink of an eye, 100,000 years, gravity and angular momentum flatten the cloud into an exploding disc, with a protostar forming in the center.
  • Clumps of dust, called chondrules, are flash-heated into droplets of molten rock and become the building blocks of the solar system.

Formation of Planets and Asteroids

  • Coaxed by gravity and turbulence, chondrules clump and grow into the first asteroids, mountains, and planets.
  • The asteroids are rubble piles of rock, metal, ice, and organics, with Bennu's parent body being a protoplanet.
  • Jupiter most likely forms near the outer edge of the solar system.

Solar System Evolution

  • About 500 million years after the sun ignites, Jupiter's massive gravity disrupts countless asteroids and comets, flinging them toward the inner planets.
  • Bennu's parent body survives this period of heavy bombardment.
  • Billions of years of quiet follow, until a billion years ago when a collision shatters the protoplanet, leading to the formation of Bennu.
  • Bennu gradually migrates toward the sun, eventually settling into a near-earth orbit.

Osiris Rex Mission

Today, NASA is sending a spacecraft called Osiris Rex to explore Bennu and retrieve a sample. Bennu has survived its long journey and settled into a near-earth orbit, bringing its secrets within our reach. Now it is ready to teach us more about the solar system’s history, its formation, its evolution, and our own place among the stars.
