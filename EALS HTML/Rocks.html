Exploring Earth's Layers and Composition

The Earth's structure is complex, consisting of various layers with distinct compositions and temperatures. Let's delve into the details:

Earth's Layers

The Earth's layers include:

  • The atmosphere, which surrounds the Earth and contains gases vital for life.
  • The crust, the Earth's solid exterior made of rocks formed over millions of years. It consists of both continental and oceanic crust.
  • The mantle, a layer beneath the crust composed of silicate minerals.
  • The core, the Earth's innermost layer, primarily made of iron and nickel.

Notably, the continental crust is different from the oceanic crust in composition and color.

Rock Formation and Transformation

Rocks play a crucial role in Earth's geological processes:

  • Igneous rocks form from the cooling and solidification of magma. They can be intrusive or extrusive.
  • Rocks undergo constant change through weathering and erosion, which break them down into sediments.
  • Sedimentary rocks form from the accumulation and compaction of sediments.
  • Sedimentary rocks can undergo further transformation into metamorphic rocks due to intense heat and pressure.
  • This continuous process of rock alteration is known as the rock cycle, involving igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks.

Bay of Bengal and Rock Transformation

The Bay of Bengal floor is covered with sediment from the Himalayas, which can undergo transformation into sedimentary rocks like sandstone and limestone.
