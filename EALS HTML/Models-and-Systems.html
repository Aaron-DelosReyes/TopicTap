Understanding Earth Systems Science

Earth Systems science helps answer complex global questions about the interactions between different Earth systems.

What are Earth Systems

Earth Systems are simplified processes of the complex Earth to aid in studying them. The four main Earth Systems are:

  • Atmosphere (air system): contains gases, regulates temperature, houses weather and wind, and drives climate systems.
  • Geosphere (land system): includes rocks, soil, continents, fossils, and tectonic plates.
  • Hydrosphere (water system): includes all salty and fresh water across the globe and its movement through the water cycle.
  • Biosphere (life system): includes all life on Earth and their interactions.

Other Earth Systems include:

  • Anthroposphere (human interactions)
  • Magnetosphere (Earth's magnetic field)
  • Exosphere (everything outside the planet)

Earth System Science

Earth Systems science combines different areas of study to understand Earth as a whole. It draws upon fields like ecology, biology, geology, meteorology, chemistry, oceanography, physics, and more. Earth Systems science is interdisciplinary, involving many scientific disciplines.

Importance of Earth System Science

Earth Systems science helps us understand how the Earth was made, its current state, and how it will change in the future. Studying human impacts on Earth Systems can inform policies and actions to preserve the planet's health.

Example of a Florida mangrove swamp wetland ecosystem:

  • Mangroves produce oxygen using sunlight, which animals need to survive.
  • Waste and decomposition introduce nutrients for plant growth.
  • Sandy bottom allows mangrove seeds to take root and trap additional sand.
  • Fresh and salt water transport sand and nutrients into the swamp.

Human activities can significantly impact all Earth's systems.

TESI (Tezi)

TESI's mission is to advance communication and education of Earth Systems science to inspire Floridians to be effective stewards of the planet. Florida is experiencing significant Earth Systems changes that threaten the environment, economy, and way of life.

Science-based information needs to be communicated effectively to help Floridians make informed decisions about natural resources and the future.
