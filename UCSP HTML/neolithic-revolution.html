LESSON I: NEOLITHIC REVOLUTION

What is the Neolithic Revolution?

NEOLITHIC REVOLUTION or so-called AGRICULTURAL REVOLUTION
The Neolithic Revolution also called the Agricultural Revolution, marked the transition in human history from small, nomadic bands of hunter-gatherers to larger, agricultural settlements and early civilization.


During the NEOLITHIC PERIOD, hunter-gatherers roamed the natural world, foraging for their food. But then a dramatic shift occurred. The foragers became farmers, transitioning from a hunter-gatherer lifestyle to a more settled one.


Complete set of Stone Tools during the Neolithic Revolution.

Agricultural Inventions During the Neolithic Revolution

  1. 1. Plant domestication

    Cereals such as emmer wheat, einkorn wheat, and barley were among the first crops domesticated by Neolithic farming communities in the Fertile Crescent. These early farmers also domesticated lentils, chickpeas, peas, and flax.

  2. 2. Livestock

    The first livestock was domesticated from animals that Neolithic humans hunted for meat. Domestic pigs were bred from wild boars, for instance, while goats came from the Persian ibex. Domesticated animals made the hard, physical labor of farming possible while their milk and meat added variety to the human diet. They also carried infectious diseases: smallpox, influenza, and measles all spread from domesticated animals to humans.

Effects of the Neolithic Revolution

The Neolithic Revolution led to masses of people establishing permanent settlements supported by farming and agriculture. It paved the way for the innovations of the ensuing Bronze age and Iron age when advancements in creating tools for farming, wars, and art swept the world and brought civilizations together through trade and conquest.

Neolithic Revolution in Different Parts of the World

  1. Middle East

    In Mesopotamia (land between rivers)

    Plant cultivation and animal domestication started in areas with reliable rainfall.

  2. Africa

    It started in Nabta Playa, Eastern Sahara, and Southern Egypt.

    It provides the early “African Cattle Complex” in which cattle are used economically for their milk and blood rather than killed. (or their beef)

  3. Europe

    In Mediterranean shores, (Greece, Italy, France)

    Shifting from foraging (searching for food) into farming imported species.

  4. Asia

    China was the first one to develop farming, based on millet rice.
