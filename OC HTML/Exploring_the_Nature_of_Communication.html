ORAL COMMUNICATION IN CONTEXT

  • - Communication is defined differently by individuals based on their perspectives.
  • - Communication involves the exchange of ideas, messages, and information between two or more persons.
  • - Communication goes beyond the mere transfer of messages and requires mutual understanding.
  • - The word "communication" comes from the Latin word "communicare," which means to share, exchange, transmit, or make common.
  • - Communication is vital for survival and helps us understand it better.

Communication is a PROCESS

  • - Communication is a process involving a source or speaker sending and encoding a message through a channel to a destination or receiver.
  • - The receiver decodes the message to provide appropriate feedback.
  • - Communication is an evolutionary process that develops and grows.
  • - Communication is dynamic and constantly changing, influenced by various factors such as the people involved, the place, the channels used, and the situation.
  • - Communication is a complex process, with one message potentially interpreted in multiple ways by different people.

Communication occurs between TWO OR MORE PEOPLE

  • - Communication occurs between two or more people acting as the speaker or receiver of the message.
  • - Communication involves interaction, such as phone conversations, face-to-face interactions, group discussions, and letter correspondence.
  • - Communication is a two-way process aimed at reaching mutual understanding, where participants exchange information and create shared meaning.
  • - Communication serves as a means of connecting people or places.

Communication can be VERBAL or NON-VERBAL, or BOTH at the same time

  • - Communication can be expressed through written or spoken words or actions.
  • - Communication involves not only facts in words but also ideas and emotions conveyed through gestures, bodily actions, facial expressions, eye behavior, and more.
  • - Communication often combines verbal and non-verbal elements, complementing each other.
  • - For example, a man winks at a lady, greets her, and offers her a drink, while the lady smirks, indicating disapproval, even without words.

Communication is INEVITABLE

  • - Communication is inevitable and omnipresent.
  • - We are constantly communicating, even when we don't intend to.
  • - Our actions, such as smiling, frowning, sitting, moving, walking, dressing, and even sleeping in class, all convey messages.

Communication is IRREVERSIBLE

  • - Communication cannot be taken back or undone once it occurs.
  • - Be careful and choose appropriate words before sending messages.

Communication is UNREPEATABLE

  • - The effect of a message changes with each repetition.
  • - The outer world, listeners, mood, and relationships change over time.
  • - You can't make the same first impression twice.
